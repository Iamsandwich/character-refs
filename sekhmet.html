Sekhmet

Sekhmet is based on the Egyptian goddess of the same name. Sekhmet looks like a young male, and is one of Ra's favourites for how kind and gentle he is.

Sekhmet can turn into a big black lion, like cats and naps on cold stone floors. He likes sweet wine and tasty meat, as well as sweet fruit and honey.

Lonely in his otherworldy palace, he told humans to bring him a friend. As one of the most devoted Sekhmet worshippers, Setepenre has passed in his old age. He was brought as a gift to Sekhmet, who accepted him in the gardens of afterlife and treated him well, feeding him, playing with him and telling him secrets of the world.

Over time Setepenre became Ma'at, a snake-bird god who now protects Sekhmet.

Bastet is one of his favourites, he calls her his kind auntie.

In an alternate version Sekhmet was put on earth in a human form to find humans worthy of saving in a very harsh enviroment, and Setepenre found him and named him Chisisi.

In a different variation both Chisisi and Setepenre are reincarnations of gods who became archeologists and Chisisi is female.

His beloved is Ma'at (Setepenre).
